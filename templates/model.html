MODEL

Loan Features

I hand pick out the features that exists in both Lending Club and Prosper and run linear regression with lasso regularization to predict the interest rate with given feature. The most important features are: Term, FICO Score, Loan Amount, Home Ownership, Dept to Income Ratio, Total Number of Accounts, Credit History, Employment Status, etc. Some of the feature engineering was done here, for example, I take the log of the annual income and reduce the number of categories of the home ownership, The credit history length is computed by the loan application date minus earliest credit line. For lending club, the joint feature is integrated into the primary application (basically boost his or hers features).

Gradient Boosting Classifer


In order to predict the loan risk, I used a gradient boosting classifier. Gradient boosting is a machine learning technique which produces a prediction model in the form of an ensemble of weak prediction models. It builds the model in a stage-wise fashion and learn from its mistake from the previous step. I chose gradient boosting for its performance. Also, investor care more about whether the loan is safe or not, rather why it is not safe. Therefore interpretability is not the most important factor here.
